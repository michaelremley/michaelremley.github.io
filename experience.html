2021

Summary

I worked on two projects in OSSTP: an interference validation tool that satellite constellations must satisfy to launch and a textbook for an Olin course called Analog and Digital Communication. Written in MATLAB, the software tool simulates the orbits of the system in question according to precession models provided by the International Telecommunications Union (ITU). While doing so, the software calculates how much interference is observed by a victim satellite on the geostationary (GSO) arc or a victim earth station via the equivalent power flux density (EPFD) metric. Satellite networks only pass the examination if they are below the statistical EPFD limits provided by the ITU.

My contributions to the EPFD validation software was the database API, orbital simulation, geometry, and EPFDup (GSO satellite victim) direction evaluation. Other members of the team were able to use shared classes and methods to perform validation for EPFDdown (GSO earth station victim). I also contributed documentation to help future team members understand the governing document of this project, ITU S.1503.

I was the project lead for the Analog and Digital Communication (ADC) textbook. With lecture slides and notes to guide the structure of the book, I wrote more than 95% of the content and created dozens of original figures. The textbook introduces mathematical tools such as the Fourier transform, convolution, and frequency-domain analysis so that students can understand and implement amplitude and frequency modulation. After simulated and real-world work with AM and FM signals, the textbook introduces statistical tools used to quantify noise, bit-error rates, power, and other important metrics of communication systems. Students spend the rest of the course learning about pulse amplitude modulation up to and including the matched filter and quadrature amplitude modulation.

In Fall 2021, I am a course assistant using the first edition textbook as an evolving teaching tool, responding to feedback from the class and others on the teaching team.
